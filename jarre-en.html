Mastabas jar

Artwork: Diorite jar

Date of discovery: 1865

Location: Mastabas of Saqqarah

Dimensions: 71 cm x 67 cm

Weight: 34 grams

Exhibition space in the museum: Egyptian Hall

These elegant, narrow-necked jars are made of diorite, the hardest stone in the world. They are empty vessels to make only a thin shell appear. However, the manufacture of the Egyptians has always been a mystery.

The place where the jars were discovered is an old Egyptian ruin where the tombs of the kings of the dynasties once were. Researchers believe that these jars existed before 2575 B.C., however there is a possibility that they might have been found in 3050 B.C. Nowadays, it is not possible to reproduce these jars, especially since experts do not know what could have been inserted in the vessel
