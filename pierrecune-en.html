Pre-cuneiform stone

Artwork: Stone/tablet

Date of Discovery: 1869

Location: Egypt

Dimensions: 12cm x 15cm

Weight: 56 grams

Exhibition space in the museum: Writing room

This pre-cuneiform stone was found in Egypt by French researchers and exported to our museum specializing in the writings of the world. We can see there the birth of a writing which is close to the cuneiform writing. The Egyptians engraved the contents themselves in order to avoid falsifications.

The inscriptions that can be seen on the stone are pre-cuneiform inscriptions. They describe an archaic Sumerian contract of sale for a field and a house.

The various hollows on the tablet signified the sale, while the crosses signed a fair contract between two people. The contract stipulates that one of the participants gives his field in exchange for a house whose value would be fair to that of a one-hectare field.
