Egyptian clay token

Artwork: Clay sphere and its tokens

Date of discovery : 1800

Location: Egyptian City

Dimensions: 73cm x 35cm

Weight: 23 grams

Exhibition space in the museum: Salle des écritures (Writing Room)

After the creation of the tablets, in order to exchange or sell their goods, the Egyptians created tokens to pay each other. These tokens are made of clay and are engraved or shaped over time and then stored in a kind of clay bubbles called "bulla".

These tokens were used for a long time but their creation gradually disappeared with the appearance of a new type of coins. The markings that can be seen on these tokens are Egyptian gods.
