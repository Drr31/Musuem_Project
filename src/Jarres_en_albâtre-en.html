Alabaster jars

Artwork: Canopic jars/vases

Date of discovery: 1816

Location: Egyptian City

Dimensions: 30cm x 13cm

Weight: 1.1kg

Exhibition space in the museum: Egyptian Hall

AAt the time, the canopic vases or jars were intended to contain the organs removed from the deceased during the mummification process. Placed in groups of four near the sarcophagus, they were made of different materials, such as limestone, alabaster, terracotta or ceramics. It was Champollion who discovered the use of these canopic jars.

These alabaster jars were explored by a team using photogrammetry with 3D laser scanners for documentation. While researchers in writing have dissected the inscriptions on these jars. Thus, the inscriptions describe the process of receiving the embalmed guts of the deceased, including a few words about the person who died.
