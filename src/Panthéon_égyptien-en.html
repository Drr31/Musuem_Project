Egyptian Pantheon

Artwork: Egyptian Pantheon

Subject: Extract from a pantheon

Place of discovery : Egypt

Date of discovery: 1908

Dimensions: 1.1 metres x 2 metres

Exhibition space in the museum: Egyptian Hall

We have here a stone extract from an Egyptian pantheon. The pantheon contained all the Egyptian gods, we recovered a piece by French archaeologists.

Thus, the figure on the front is Anubis, the jackal-headed jackal god. His role is to watch over the deceased and accompany them to judgment before Osiris, who is in charge of mummification.

Above him we have symbols showing his role in every detail. First Anubis welcomes the dead, then mummifies them with Osiris to make them rotproof and eternal. In particular, he purifies the heart and the entrails that they say are "soiled" by earthly abjections.
