Contract stone

Artwork: Stele

Date of discovery: 1799

Location: Egypt

Dimensions: 117.2 cm x 62.7 cm x 28.1 cm

Weight: 821 grams

Exhibition space in the museum: Salle des Ã©critures (Writing Room)

This stone comes from a fragment of a stele engraved in Ancient Egypt. It displays three versions of the same text that allowed the decoding of hieroglyphs in the 19th century.

The text clarifies a contract between Memphis and Pharaoh Ptolemy V. Written in two languages and three different scripts it has allowed a major step forward in the deciphering of demotic Egypt and the Greek alphabet.

This stone was unique at the time but is unfortunately no longer unique today, as other stones have been discovered and are on display in other museums. However, its role has been essential in the modern understanding of the ancient civilization.
