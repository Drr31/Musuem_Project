Sarcophagus panel

Artwork: Sarcophagus panel

Subject: Piece of an empty Egyptian sarcophagus

Date of Discovery: Between 1872 and 1879

Dimensions: 60 cm x 32 cm

Weight: 312 grams

Exhibition space in the museum: Egyptian Hall

In ancient Egypt, sarcophagi were used to collect corpses or coffins.

This panel includes some extracts from drawings which tell us about the arrival of the deceased in the world of death close to the Gods, including Anubis. The sarcophagus carried by a bull signifies the rising of the sarcophagus to Isis. At the top of the panel, we can see the funeral goddess Queen Isis. With her wings spread, she waits for the soul of the dead man to decide his destiny.
