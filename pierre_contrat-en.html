Book of the Dead Papyrus

Artwork: Egyptian Papyrus

Discovery date: 1887

Location: Egypt

Dimensions: 75cm x 44cm

Weight: Variable

Exhibition space in the museum: Salle des Ã©critures (Writing Room)

This manuscript was discovered by a team of French and Egyptian archaeologists south of Cairo in 2011. Found in approximately 500 pieces, the papyrus has been reconstructed and is now on display in our museum.

It evokes the construction of the Great Pyramid of Giza west of Cairo. More specifically, it shows that workers who took part in the construction of the pyramid, which is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

However, even before the discovery of papyrus, few details concerning the reign of the pharaoh of Dynasty IV were available. So, this manuscript stipulates that the reign of Pharaoh Cheops would have been well over 26 years according to Mr. Mahfouz, a researcher in pharaonic language.
